Moving to Mexico? Why the Xoloitzcuintle is the Perfect Expat Dog

By Xolos Ramirez Published on June 1, 2026

Relocating to Mexico is a dream for many. With its vibrant culture, incredible cuisine, and beautiful landscapes, it’s no wonder expats flock here. But as you pack your bags and prepare for a new chapter south of the border, you might be considering adding a furry friend to your new household. Look no further than the native Xoloitzcuintle (pronounced show-low-eats-QUEENT-lee), or "Xolo" for short.

Often referred to as the Mexican Hairless Dog, the Xolo is much more than just an exotic pet. Here is why the Xoloitzcuintle is the absolute perfect companion for your new life in Mexico.

1. A Deep Cultural Connection

If you want to immerse yourself in Mexican history, the Xolo is your living link. These dogs have been around for over 3,000 years and were considered sacred by the Aztecs and Mayans. They were believed to be spiritual guides that helped souls journey through the underworld. Walking a Xolo through the colorful streets of your new neighborhood isn't just a stroll; it’s an incredible conversation starter and a bridge to connecting with locals who hold deep respect for this national treasure.

2. Built for the Climate

Mexico's climate can be beautifully warm, but it can also be tough on heavily coated breeds like Huskies or Golden Retrievers. The hairless variety of the Xoloitzcuintle is perfectly adapted to the Mexican heat. Their lack of fur means they don't overheat easily, making them fantastic companions for coastal living or the warm valleys of the interior. Just remember: while they can handle the heat, their exposed skin requires dog-safe sunscreen during peak sunny hours!

3. Low Maintenance and Clean

Whether you are renting a chic apartment in Roma Norte or a beachfront villa in Tulum, you want to keep your space looking pristine. Xolos are incredibly clean dogs. The hairless variety obviously does not shed, which means no dog hair on your furniture or clothes. They also lack the typical "doggy odor" that furred dogs develop, and fleas are rarely an issue since there's no fur to hide in.

💡 Note on Varieties: Did you know Xolos come in three sizes (Toy, Miniature, and Standard) and two coat varieties (Hairless and Coated)? Even the coated Xolos have short, easy-to-manage fur!

4. Loyal and Protective Companions

Moving to a new country can sometimes feel isolating. Xolos are renowned for their intense loyalty and affectionate nature toward their families. Often described as "Velcro dogs," they form a very deep bond with their owners. Furthermore, they are naturally alert and make excellent, sensible watchdogs. They aren't overly aggressive, but they will certainly let you know if a stranger is approaching your new home, offering peace of mind.

5. A Healthy, Primitive Breed

Because the Xolo is a "primitive" breed—meaning their evolution was largely shaped by natural selection rather than intense human breeding—they are generally incredibly healthy and robust. They have a long lifespan (often 14-20 years) and are not prone to many of the genetic diseases that plague highly engineered modern breeds. For an expat still figuring out the local veterinary landscape, a naturally hardy dog is a huge blessing.

The Verdict

Choosing to move to Mexico is an adventure of a lifetime. Bringing a Xoloitzcuintle into your life ensures that your adventure is shared with a loyal, historically rich, and perfectly adapted companion. Embrace the culture, enjoy the weather, and let a Xolo show you the true heart of Mexico.
